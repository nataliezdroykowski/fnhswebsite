WHAT IS FRENCH NHS?

French National Honor Society is a student-run organization for advanced French students at the Woodlands High School (French III and higher). Members of French NHS participate in various activities to enhance their understanding of the French language and culture and to spread this knowledge throughout the school. We assist with volunteering events, help tutor other French students, host school-wide cultural events and socials, and hold roundtables to practice our French. Some of our most popular events are Fear Factor, Meet Your Match, and Thank a Teacher. Our primary goal is to get more students to study French!

CLUB INFO

  • General meetings take place on the second Monday of each month (unless told otherwise) in room 220
  • Our club sponsor is Madame Whiteman, who teaches Accelerated French, French III, and French AP
  • The focus of FNHS for this year is to recruit more middle schoolers and high schoolers to take French and other non-Spanish languages, so we will be promoting French classes and French club to new and future students
  • To bring greater awareness to the cultures of the various francophone countries around the world, we will be giving monthly roundtables and meetings the theme of different countries
  • French NHS is also in charge of French Symposium (meetings, practice, etc), which is an enriching competition for French students state-wide
  • We have elections at the end of the year for all incoming French III and French AP students (current French II and French III)
