TEXAS FRENCH SYMPOSIUM

See the official website for Texas French Symposium HERE
  • Texas French Symposium (TFS) is a statewide competition for high school students studying French
  • The goal of this competition is to encourage French students to improve their oral and cultural competencies and to promote French language learning of high school students across the state
  • Students have the opportunity to compete in 29 different categories, including written, oral, and art events
  • Awards are given for achievement to individual student winners, school teams, and the top ten ranking schools

EVENTS

  • Art - Students will create art entries before the competition that reflect the francophone culture and follow the current year’s TFS theme
    • Categories: Pencil/Charcoal, Pastel/Colored Pencil, Acrylic Painting, Oil Painting, Watercolor, Digital Media, 3D Artwork
  • BAC - Teams of 4 members will answer questions related to France or French aloud through buzzing in and attempting to answer
  • Band Dessinée - This is the comic strip where a maximum of 2 students will design a comic strip in French
  • Dictée - The dictée passage will be read 3 times, and students will attempt to write down what is being said
  • Drama Group - Groups of 2-8 students will have 5-10 minutes to perform a memorized francophone play
  • Extemporaneous Speaking - Students will respond orally to a given written prompt with 3 minutes to prepare and 3 minutes to respond to the topic
  • Instrumental Solo - Students will play instrumental selections by a recognized francophone composer with a time limit of 5 minutes or less
  • Poetry - Students will have 3 minutes to recite a memorized poem by a francophone author
  • Prose - Students will perform a selected francophone prose with a time limit of 3 minutes
  • Sightreading - Students will read aloud a provided passage after 3 minutes to read over it and prepare
  • Testing - All students participating in TFS will take interpretive reading and listening test which are in multiple-choice format
  • Vocal Group - A 5 minute group performance of a song in French with a recorded accompaniment or a cappella
  • Vocal Solo - A 5 minute solo performance of a song in French with a recorded accompaniment or a cappella
